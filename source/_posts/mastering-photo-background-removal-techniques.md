## Part 2: How to Blur Photo Background?

A blurred background helps bring your image into focus. If you want a photo editor to blur background of your image, you can try Photoshop. Here are the steps for that:

Step 1: Isolate Subject

Isolate your subject on a separate layer by accessing the ‚ÄúQuick Selection‚Äù tool and clicking on the ‚ÄúSelect Subject‚Äù button. You can use the "Lasso" tool to refine your selection. Click on "Select and Mask" and select the ‚ÄúBlack and White View.‚Äù Now, choose the output to "New Layer with Layer Mask."

![change the output](https://images.wondershare.com/filmora/article-images/2022/edit-blur-or-remove-photo-background-7.jpg)

Step 2: Expand the Selection

Hold the ‚ÄúCTRL‚Äù key and select your layer mask. Go to the "Select" tab, find and expand "Modify," and tap "Expand." Select a suitable number and tap ‚ÄúOK‚Äù Next, go to the ‚ÄúEdit‚Äù tab and select ‚ÄúContent-Aware Fill."

![expand selection by pixels](https://images.wondershare.com/filmora/article-images/2022/edit-blur-or-remove-photo-background-8.jpg)

Step 3: Apply Photo Blur

Right-click on the background layer and choose "Convert to Smart Object." Head over to the "Filter" tab, expand "Blur Gallery," and choose "Tilt-Shift." You can change the field lines to adjust the depth of field for your image and create a smooth transition. You can also control the intensity of the blur from the slider present on the right. Click ‚ÄúOK‚Äù and save your image.
